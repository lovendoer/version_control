About the City
The Sugar City of
Negros Occidental

Kabankalan City is the second-largest city in Negros Occidental, known for its thriving sugarcane industry, breathtaking natural scenery, and rich cultural traditions. From the misty highlands of Barangay Oringao to the lively streets during the Sinulog Festival, Kabankalan offers a diverse and authentic Filipino experience.

🌿
Natural Wonders
Home to cascading waterfalls, lush eco-parks, and serene rivers tucked within tropical forest.
🎉
Living Culture
Vibrant festivals, local markets, and Spanish colonial heritage define the city's identity.
🏙️
City of Commerce
A key economic hub in southern Negros, driven by sugar, agriculture, and local trade.
🤝
Warm Hospitality
Kabankaleños are known for their warmth and pride in sharing their city with visitors.
Getting Here
How to Get to
Kabankalan

Kabankalan City is accessible by land and sea from major cities in the Visayas. Here are the most common ways to reach the city.

✈️
By Air
Fly into Bacolod–Silay Airport, then take a bus or van south to Kabankalan (approx. 2 hrs).
🚌
By Bus
Regular buses depart from Bacolod City to Kabankalan via the Kabankalan highway daily.
⛴️
By Sea
Ferry from Cebu to Dumaguete or Bacolod, then connect via bus or van to Kabankalan.
🚗
By Car
Drive south from Bacolod along the national highway. Kabankalan is about 90 km from the city.
